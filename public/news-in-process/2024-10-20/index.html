October 20, 2024

There are two main approaches for evaluating investments and making trading decisions: fundamental analysis and technical analysis. See Investopedia.

The same can be said for political analysis and election forecasting.

All is well when fundamental and technical analyses agree, when the political wisdom of the pundits aligns with the data from polls and prediction markets. This is not a time of alignment.

Fundamental analysis draws parallels between what is happening today and previous elections. Much of what we know about past elections points to a Democratic victory on election day, November 5, 2024.

Allan Lichtman’s (2024) Keys to the White House is predicting a victory for the incumbent Democratic Party, and he is sticking with his prediction. Lichtman takes a fundamental approach to election forecasting, as described in a NewsNation interview from October 7. He does not change his predictions based on poll results.

In the October 2 news posting, we showed a machine learning analysis of historical factors, suggesting that the Harris/Walz ticket has an 83.3% chance of winning in the Electoral College. This is technical analysis in support of fundamental analysis.

As early as August 5–11, we pointed to similarities between 2024 and 1964. Donald Trump, a radical, right-wing Republican candidate, is running against Kamala Harris, a center-left Democratic candidate. In 1964, a Democratic ticket lead by Lyndon Johnson won decisively with 61.1 percent of the popular vote and 486 Electoral College votes. Barry Goldwater, a radical far-right Republican senator from Arizona, won his home state and a few states in the deep South, for a total of only 52 Electoral College votes.

The Democratic Party’s campaign slogan in 1964, referring to Goldwater: “In your guts you know he’s nuts.” Johnson’s words from the infamous Daisy Ad: “We must either love each other, or we must die.” Must the Democrats take a hard-hitting “Daisy Ad” approach to win in November?

On the campaign trail throughout October, Kamala Harris has proven to be significantly more effective than Donald Trump. The October 19 news posting points to Harris’s talent as a communicator. Fundamental analysis of candidate speech suggests a Democratic victory.

Traditional campaign advice suggests that candidates must move toward the political center in order to win a general election. In recent interview on Hamodia, when asked “What do you think each candidate can do to improve his/her chances?”, we replied, “Trump needs to be less focused on Harris’s identity. To make this election about race or gender is a mistake. He needs to broaden his base, move beyond white males, and be more open to the diversity of America.” Trump has done none of that. Instead he has demeaned others. His words are divisive, hateful, even cruel. Trump has focused on the MAGA base alone.

Harris, on the other hand, has welcomed all citizens into the Democratic fold, regardless of ethnic background or previous political affiliation. She aspires to be a president who will serve all the people.

Fundamental analysis would suggest that Harris’s centrist strategy would win over Trump’s MAGA-only strategy.

Trump has the trappings of a flawed candidate. He is a convicted felon, a failed businessman, and an abuser of women. In the August 26–31 news posting we asked, “Will Trump’s personality be his undoing?”

Trump’s actions on January 6, 2021, seem disqualifying. In the New Yorker of February 17, 2020, Mitt Romney words were: “Corrupting an election to keep oneself in office is perhaps the most abusive and destructive violation of one’s oath of office that I can imagine.” Hundreds of Republican’s have rejected Trump in 2024, many have endorsed Harris for president.

Personal character matters to voters. Harris wins on character.

Candidate likeability matters to voters. Without debate, Harris beats Trump on likeability.
